# Payment and net-income design for a mainland China seller

Updated 2026-06-23. Fees can change; verify the rates and payout methods displayed in your own account.

## Recommended route: Fiverr + Bank Transfer via Payoneer

Launch price: **$65 per order**.

Using Fiverr’s current 80% seller earnings rule:

- Net platform earnings per order: $52
- Two orders: $104
- Fiverr currently lists a $1 Bank Transfer withdrawal fee with a $20 minimum
- Withdrawing the two orders together: approximately $103 before tax, exchange-rate spread, or other provider charges

## Upwork

Launch price: **$65 per order**.

Upwork’s current freelancer service fee is 0%-15% per contract and is shown before accepting/submitting the contract.

- At the worst-case 15%, one order nets $55.25
- Two orders net $110.50 before payout, exchange-rate, and tax costs
- If expected payout costs exceed $10.50, quote $69 instead

## Direct PayPal clients

Direct price: **$75 per order**, with two orders accumulated before one withdrawal where practical.

PayPal currently publishes a 4.40% + $0.30 fee for international commercial payments received by mainland China accounts, plus a $35 fee for a USD wire withdrawal to a mainland bank account.

- $75 after payment fee is approximately $71.40
- Two payments total approximately $142.80
- One $35 withdrawal leaves approximately $107.80 before conversion, intermediary-bank, or tax costs

Do not misclassify commercial payments as friends-and-family payments to avoid fees.

## Public pricing line

> The launch price is $65 through Fiverr or Upwork. For direct cross-border invoicing, the price is $75 because of payment and withdrawal costs.
